Goal: Understand how the humerus relates to the body as a whole.

Purpose: To encourage deeper, longer-lasting learning by linking the bone to the skeletal, muscular, and cardiovascular systems rather than relying on rote memorization.

How to use this guide: Follow the five structured steps below. They are designed to help you focus on meaningful connections rather than just memorizing facts.

Step 1 – Location

Upper Arm

Step 2 – Shape

Long Bone - functions as a lever that enables movement of the arm

Step 3 – Neighbors

  • Humerus + Scapula

    Glenohumeral Joint

    The humerus's head articulates with the scapula's glenoid fossa, forming the glenohumeral joint. This synovial ball-and-socket joint allows circumduction and rotation. The humeral head is rounded like a ball, while the glenoid fossa is concave like a cup.

  • Humeroradial

    Part of Elbow Joint

    The humerus's capitulum articulates with the radius's head, forming a pivot joint that allows hinging and rotation. The radius head is disk-shaped, while the humeral capitulum is rounded.

  • Humeroulnar

    Part of Elbow Joint

    The humerus's trochlea articulates with the ulna's trochlear notch, forming a hinge joint that allows flexion and extension of the forearm. The ulna’s notch is C-shaped and wraps around the humeral trochlea.

Step 4 – Anatomical Landmarks

Head and Neck

  • Head of Humerus
    • Articulates with glenoid fossa of scapula to form shoulder (glenohumeral) joint
  • Anatomical Neck of Humerus
    • Narrow region distal to head
    • Attachment site for shoulder joint capsule
  • Surgical Neck of Humerus
    • Narrow region distal to tubercles
    • Common fracture site
    • Close to axillary nerve and anterior/posterior circumflex humeral arteries
  • Axillary nerve
    • Sends motor signals to deltoid and teres minor
      • Deltoid: arm flexion/extension, medial/lateral rotation, abduction
      • Teres minor: lateral rotation and adduction of arm
    • Brings sensory signals from skin over lateral shoulder
    • Significance: if damaged, may lose motor control of deltoid and sensation of lateral shoulder
  • Anterior circumflex humeral artery
    • Supplies blood to deltoid, teres minor, teres major, coracobrachialis, shoulder joint, head of humerus
    • Muscle actions:
      • Deltoid: arm flexion/extension, medial/lateral rotation, abduction
      • Teres minor: lateral rotation and adduction
      • Teres major: adducts, medially rotates, extends flexed arm
      • Coracobrachialis: flexes and adducts arm at shoulder
  • Posterior circumflex humeral artery
    • Supplies blood to deltoid, teres minor, teres major, long/lateral heads of triceps brachii, shoulder joint, surrounding tissues
    • Muscle actions:
      • Deltoid: arm flexion/extension, medial/lateral rotation, abduction
      • Teres minor: lateral rotation and adduction
      • Teres major: adducts, medially rotates, extends flexed arm
      • Triceps brachii: extends forearm at elbow

Tubercles

  • Intertubercular Sulcus (Bicipital groove)
    • Guides long tendon of biceps brachii
    • Insertion site for latissimus dorsi tendon
      • Latissimus dorsi: adducts, medially rotates, extends arm
  • Greater Tubercle
    • Lateral projection between anatomical and surgical neck
    • Insertion site for supraspinatus, infraspinatus, teres minor (rotator cuff)
      • Supraspinatus: abduction of arm
      • Infraspinatus: lateral rotation of arm
      • Teres minor: lateral rotation and adduction of arm
    • Covered by deltoid muscle to form rounded shoulder
  • Crest of Greater Tubercle
    • Lateral lip of intertubercular sulcus
    • Insertion site for pectoralis major tendon
      • Pectoralis major: adducts, medially rotates, flexes/extends humerus
  • Lesser Tubercle
    • Anterior projection between anatomical and surgical neck
    • Insertion site for subscapularis (rotator cuff)
      • Subscapularis: medial rotation of arm
  • Crest of Lesser Tubercle
    • Medial lip of intertubercular sulcus
    • Insertion site for teres major tendon
      • Teres major: adducts, medially rotates, extends flexed arm
  • Anterior circumflex humeral artery
    • Supplies blood to deltoid, teres minor, teres major, coracobrachialis, shoulder joint, head of humerus
    • Muscle actions:
      • Deltoid: arm flexion/extension, medial/lateral rotation, abduction
      • Teres minor: lateral rotation and adduction
      • Teres major: adducts, medially rotates, extends flexed arm
      • Coracobrachialis: flexes and adducts arm at shoulder
  • Posterior circumflex humeral artery
    • Supplies blood to deltoid, teres minor, teres major, long/lateral heads of triceps brachii, shoulder joint, surrounding tissues
    • Muscle actions:
      • Deltoid: arm flexion/extension, medial/lateral rotation, abduction
      • Teres minor: lateral rotation and adduction
      • Teres major: adducts, medially rotates, extends flexed arm
      • Triceps brachii: extends forearm at elbow
